Bonsai Website Redesign

Student Project

This was a five weeks real website practical assignment. Aiming to redesign a website and improve the users experience. I chose a website called Bonsai Direct, which was a e-commerce website. Due to the time constrain, I chose just redesign the homepage and the shopping process. In order to improve the users experience, solve the problems. I need to figure out where the problems lie, then how can I solve them. Furthermore, find a elegant solution to those problems through design thinking.

Delivarables

Visual branding
UI & UX design

Years

2020

Objective

Redesign website to encourage meaningful engagement, reducing the bounce rate, and increasing the traffic.

Compare - Preview Browse and preview at the same time

The differences between old and new design are the visual effect and content layout.


Problems :

First, based on the feedbacks I collected from participants, main problem of old website was there were massive promotions which resulted in turn to distract users attention. The cluttered layout which lead meaningful engagement was very low. Second, a lot of categories were placed in the navigation bar, and I found that some categories were kind of needless duplication. So to avoid visually clutter, I reduced some not that important repeated entrances in the nav bar. Last, reduced the number of shopping steps to retain users.


Solutions :

I was expecting to improved it visually and functionally. First, I redesigned a new logo to make it more adaptable to different devices and reorganized the navigation bar, as well as reduced some repeated promotions to two large-space part as the engagement area to accommodate the marketing needs.

STEP - 1 Research & Define basic idea

Targeted market research, created and analyzed personas which be recognised three target audiences types. Then got moodboard for inspiration, a clean, adaptable, simplicity style brand identity was designed.

Understading Problems :

As a fan of bonsai, I understood the pains of how to care for the trees and where I could get a unique bonsai to decorate my home. I wanted to validate these problems with others who also were interested in bonsai, so I took time find 7 random friends and classmates to went them through the original website and ask them where they would be frustrated.




After listening to the participants, I prioritized the top frustrations:

  • Clutter layout and low-quality images. Some people were expecting to plant a bonsai, which is elegant and special. but the clutter layout and the low-quality images make the first impression for that website badly, even wanted click exit right away.
  • Shopping process confusion. The shopping policy was very clear, but some participants said they sometimes wanted to order the tree immediately, but they always need to "add to basket" first, and then they could find the "place order" button. Furthermore, the "place order" page had massive informations needed to fill, which lead some users impatient.
  • Needs of professional plant courses. The users were most potentially tend to care for the bonsai professionally. From the feedback, most visitors said they found the teaching videos hidden deeply lead them to think there's no video course supported.

    After the ideation of sticky notes, I redesigned the following key features:

  • Reorganized the content categories. Sum up the pain points of the visitors, I visually redesigned the whole page to convey a elegant modern style and reduce some repeated catogories and drag the courses part into the navigation bar instead of hidden deeply.
  • Added a "buy now" botton. On the item detail description page, I planed to add a "buy now" botton above the "add to basket" to reduce a step of shopping process. After defined sitemap and usage scense, user flow diagram came out, Low and High-fidelity prototype were designed use XD.
