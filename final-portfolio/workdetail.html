Bonsai Website Redesign

Student Project

For this project, I spent 12 hours per week, which lasted five weeks. Aiming to redesign a website and improve the users' experience. I chose a website called Bonsai Direct, which was a e-commerce website. Due to the time constrain, I chose just to redesign the homepage and the shopping process. In order to improve the users' experience, solve the problems. I need to figure out where the problems lie, then how can I solve them. Furthermore, find a elegant solution to those problems through design thinking.

Delivarables

Visual branding
UI & UX design

Years

2020

Objective

Redesign website to encourage meaningful engagement, reducing the bounce rate, and increasing the traffic.

Compare - Preview Browse and preview at the same time

Old page have massive clutted images are full of distractions, which lead meaningful engagement was very low. Other than that, navigation bar have some needless duplication. New page solved the pain point of the users.

STEP - 1 Research & Define basic idea

Targeted market research, analyzed personas, based on the inspirations, a clean, adaptable new brand identity was designed.

Understading Problems :

As a fan of bonsai, I understood the pain of frastrating with how to care for the bonsais and where to get unique one. I wanted to validate these problems with others, so I took time find 9 random friends and classmates to walk them through the original website and asked where they would be frustrated.




After listening to the participants, I prioritized the top frustrations:

  • Clutter layout and low-quality images. Some people were expecting to plant a bonsai, which is elegant and special. but the clutter layout and the low-quality images make the first impression for that website badly, even wanted to click exit right away.
  • Shopping process confusion. The shopping policies are very clear, but some participants said that they sometimes wanted to order the tree instantly, but they always need to "add to basket" first, and then, the "place order" button could appears. Furthermore, the "place order" page had massive informations needed to fill, which cause some users impatient.
  • Needs of professional plant courses. The users were most potentially tend to care for the bonsai professionally. From the feedback, most visitors said they found the teaching videos hidden deeply lead them to think there's no video course supported.

    • STEP - 2 Ideation & Prototype

    After the ideation of sticky notes, I redesigned the following key features:

  • Reorganized the content categories. Analyzed the common pain points of the visitors, I Functionally reduced some repeated catogories and dragged the courses part into the navigation bar as opposed to hidden them deeply. I planed to visually redesign the whole page to convey a elegant modern style for users.
  • Added a "buy now" botton. On the item detail description page, I added a "buy now" botton above the "add to basket" to conveinient the users who wanted to buy directly, this botton could reduce a redundant step of the shopping process.

    • After defined sitemap and usage scense, user flow diagram was drew and Low-fidelity prototype were designed using Adobe XD.

    STEP - 3 Visualized

    Defined visual rules to limite clutter, fine-tuning all pages with consistency style assets.

    STEP - 4 Responsive display

    Responsive design is friendly for users to access on different devices.
